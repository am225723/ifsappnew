# Assessment Recording Scripts

These are narration scripts for audio introductions to each assessment. Currently, only the **Attachment Style Assessment** has a recorded intro (`attachment-assessment-intro.mp3`).

After recording, place each MP3 file in `public/audio/assessments/` using the suggested filename.

---

## 1. IFS Wound Assessment
**Filename:** `wound-assessment-intro.mp3`

> Welcome to the IFS Wound Assessment. This assessment will help you discover which inner child wounds may be affecting your life today.
>
> In Internal Family Systems, we recognize five core childhood wounds: abandonment, shame, neglect, betrayal, and helplessness. Most of us carry at least one of these wounds, and they shape how our protective parts operate.
>
> You'll be presented with 25 statements. For each one, rate how strongly it resonates with you on a scale from 1, meaning "not at all," to 5, meaning "very strongly."
>
> There are no right or wrong answers. This is about understanding your inner world with curiosity and compassion — not judging yourself. Your protector parts developed for very good reasons, and this assessment helps us understand what they've been protecting.
>
> After the main questions, you'll also be asked about five common protector patterns. These help identify which protective parts are most active in your system.
>
> Take your time with each question. If a statement brings up strong feelings, notice that with kindness — it may mean a part is responding. You can pause at any time.
>
> When you're ready, begin with the first statement.

## 2. Identify Your Protective Parts
**Filename:** `parts-assessment-intro.mp3`

> Welcome to the Protective Parts Assessment. This assessment helps you identify which protective parts are most active in your inner system.
>
> In IFS, we recognize three types of parts. Manager parts are proactive protectors — they try to prevent pain through control, planning, and perfectionism. Firefighter parts are reactive protectors — they numb or distract when pain surfaces, often through impulsive behaviors. And exile parts are the young, vulnerable parts that carry the original wounds of pain, shame, fear, and loneliness.
>
> You'll see 15 statements describing common experiences. Rate each one from 1, meaning "not at all like me," to 5, meaning "very much like me."
>
> Remember, there are no bad parts. Every part of you developed for a reason — to protect you in some way. This assessment isn't about labeling parts as good or bad, but about getting to know your inner family with curiosity and compassion.
>
> As you answer, you might notice certain parts reacting to the questions. That's completely normal. Just notice them with warmth and continue at your own pace.
>
> When you're ready, begin with the first statement.

## 3. Self-Energy Assessment
**Filename:** `self-energy-assessment-intro.mp3`

> Welcome to the Self-Energy Assessment. This assessment evaluates your current connection to the eight qualities of Self — what we call the 8 C's in Internal Family Systems.
>
> These eight qualities are: Calmness — the ability to remain centered even in stress. Curiosity — genuine interest in understanding your experiences without judgment. Compassion — warmth and kindness toward yourself and your parts. Confidence — trust in your ability to handle whatever arises. Courage — willingness to face fears and take steps toward healing. Clarity — seeing situations clearly without being clouded by parts. Creativity — thinking flexibly and finding novel solutions. And Connectedness — feeling connected to others and to something larger than yourself.
>
> You'll be presented with 16 statements, two for each quality. Rate each one from 1, meaning "rarely true for me," to 5, meaning "almost always true for me."
>
> This assessment measures how much access you currently have to your Self energy. Lower scores in certain areas simply show where parts may be blending with you more strongly — and that's information, not judgment.
>
> Your Self energy is always there, even when parts are activated. This assessment helps you see where your Self shines through most clearly and where there's room to grow.
>
> Take your time and answer honestly. When you're ready, begin.

## 4. Attachment Style Assessment
**Filename:** `attachment-assessment-intro.mp3` *(already recorded)*

> Welcome to the Attachment Style Assessment. This assessment helps you understand your relationship patterns and how your attachment style influences the way you connect with others.
>
> Attachment theory identifies four main styles. Secure attachment means you're comfortable with both intimacy and independence, and you trust others and yourself. Anxious-preoccupied attachment means you crave closeness but fear rejection, and you tend to be hypervigilant to relationship cues. Dismissive-avoidant attachment means you value independence highly and feel uncomfortable with vulnerability or emotional closeness. And fearful-avoidant, sometimes called disorganized attachment, means you desire closeness but fear it, oscillating between approach and withdrawal.
>
> You'll see 20 statements about how you experience relationships. Rate each one from 1, meaning "not at all like me," to 5, meaning "very much like me."
>
> Most people have elements of multiple styles, and your attachment style can shift over time with awareness and healing work. There's no "bad" style — each one developed as an adaptive response to your early relationships.
>
> Understanding your attachment patterns is a powerful step in your IFS journey, because your attachment style often reveals which exile parts carry the deepest wounds and which protectors are working hardest.
>
> Answer at your own pace. When you're ready, begin with the first statement.
