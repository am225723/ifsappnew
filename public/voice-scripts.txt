A1. MEETING YOUR SELF
Filename: meeting-self.mp3
Duration: 15 minutes
Type: Guided Meditation (Beginner)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Welcome to this meditation to meet your Self.

Begin by finding a comfortable position — sitting or lying down, whatever feels right for you. Allow your eyes to gently close. Take a slow, deep breath in through your nose... and release it through your mouth with a soft sigh.

Take another deep breath in... filling your lungs completely... and exhale slowly, letting your shoulders drop and your body soften.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now bring your attention to your breath. Don't try to change it — simply notice it. The gentle rise and fall of your chest. The cool air entering your nostrils. The warm air leaving.

[Pause 10 seconds]

As you settle into this quiet space, I want you to know something important: within you, beneath all the busy thoughts, the worries, the roles you play, and the emotions that come and go — there is a core part of you that is always present. This is your Self. It isn't something you need to create or develop. It has always been here, waiting for you to turn inward and notice it.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Your Self carries eight natural qualities: Curiosity — an open, non-judgmental wondering about your experience. Compassion — a tender warmth toward yourself and all your parts. Calm — a spacious stillness that can hold any emotion without being swept away. Clarity — the ability to see your inner world with gentle precision. Confidence — a quiet knowing that you can handle whatever arises. Courage — the willingness to face difficult feelings rather than turn away. Creativity — the capacity to find new ways of healing and growing. And Connectedness — a felt sense that all parts of you belong.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Right now, as you breathe, I invite you to simply notice: is there a quality of openness present? Perhaps a gentle curiosity about what you're experiencing? If you can notice that curiosity — that IS your Self. You don't have to do anything special. Simply noticing is enough.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Imagine that your Self is like the sky — vast, clear, and unchanging. Your thoughts are like clouds passing through. Your emotions are like weather — sometimes stormy, sometimes calm. But the sky itself is always there, always spacious, always holding everything with equanimity.

[Pause 10 seconds]

From this place of spacious awareness, allow yourself to greet your inner world. You might say silently: "Hello. I'm here. I'm listening." Notice what happens inside when you offer this greeting. Perhaps a part of you relaxes slightly. Perhaps you feel a subtle warmth in your chest. Perhaps nothing obvious happens, and that's perfectly fine too.

[Pause 15 seconds]

As you rest in this connection with your Self, place one hand gently over your heart. Feel the warmth of your palm. Feel your heartbeat beneath your hand. This heart has been beating for you since before you were born. It is faithful, tireless, and always present — just like your Self.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now imagine sending a gentle wave of compassion from your hand into your heart. Let it spread through your chest, down into your belly, up into your shoulders, and throughout your entire body. You might say to yourself: "I am here for all of me. Every part of me is welcome."

[Pause 20 seconds]

As we begin to close this meditation, I invite you to create a simple anchor you can use throughout your day to reconnect with your Self. Perhaps pressing your thumb and forefinger together, or placing your hand on your heart, or taking one specific deep breath. Choose a gesture that feels meaningful to you.

Practice this anchor now: make the gesture, feel the Self energy, and release. Do this three times.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Take three slow, grounding breaths. With each inhale, draw your Self energy deeper into your body. With each exhale, release any remaining tension.

Feel your feet on the floor or the surface beneath you. Wiggle your fingers and toes. Notice the sounds around you. Feel the temperature of the air on your skin.

[Pause 10 seconds]

When you're ready, gently open your eyes. Take a moment to look around and orient yourself. Notice how you feel — perhaps lighter, more spacious, more grounded.

Your Self is always here. It never leaves. Whenever you feel lost in the noise of life, simply pause, use your anchor, and turn inward. Your Self is waiting, always ready to lead with wisdom, compassion, and love.

Welcome home to your Self.

A2. CULTIVATING SELF QUALITIES
Filename: self-qualities.mp3
Duration: 20 minutes
Type: Guided Practice (Intermediate)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Today we'll explore the qualities of your Self. Each quality is already within you — like seeds that have been planted and are waiting for the sunlight of your attention to help them bloom.

Find a comfortable position and allow your eyes to close. Take three deep breaths to arrive in this moment.

[Pause 10 seconds]

We'll explore each of the eight qualities of Self, one at a time. As we move through each one, allow yourself to feel it in your body, see it in your mind's eye, and let it gently fill your being.

Let us begin with Curiosity. Imagine a warm, golden light glowing softly in the center of your forehead. This light represents your capacity for open, non-judgmental wondering. Curiosity doesn't ask "Why am I like this?" from frustration — it asks "I wonder what this part is trying to tell me?" with genuine warmth. Feel this light expanding, touching each thought with a welcoming glow. Whisper internally: "I am curious about all of my experience."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Now feel that golden light softening into a warm, rose-pink glow that settles in your heart center. This is Compassion — tender, unconditional love flowing from your Self toward every part of you, especially those who are hurting. Place your hand gently over your heart. Compassion says: "I see your pain. It matters. You matter." Allow this soft light to expand with each breath, flowing through your chest and down your arms. There is no part of you unworthy of this compassion.

[Pause 20 seconds]

Notice how a gentle, cool, silvery-blue light begins to rise from the base of your spine. This is Calm — not the absence of emotion, but the spacious capacity to be present with whatever arises. Imagine yourself as a deep, clear mountain lake. Storms may move across the surface, but in the depths, all is still and undisturbed. Let this calm deepen with each exhale.

[Pause 20 seconds]

From within this deep calm, notice a clear, crystalline white light emerging behind your eyes, like a fog gently lifting to reveal a vast, clear sky. This is Clarity — the ability to see your experience without being merged with it. With clarity, you can see that your protective parts are not enemies but loyal guardians. Your wounded parts are not broken — they are carrying burdens that were never theirs.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Feel a steady, warm amber light glowing in your solar plexus. This is Confidence — not arrogance, but a deep, quiet knowing that you have within you everything your parts need. This light pulses gently with each heartbeat, affirming: "I can handle this. I have the wisdom, the love, and the patience to be with my parts."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Sense a bright, fiery orange-red light kindling in the center of your chest. This is Courage — the willingness to face what is difficult, to turn toward pain rather than away. Courage is not the absence of fear — it is the choice to be present despite fear. Feel this light giving you the strength to say to your most wounded parts: "Show me. I can handle it. I will not look away."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Notice a playful, shimmering violet-purple light beginning to dance around the crown of your head. This is Creativity — the capacity to envision new possibilities, to find fresh solutions, to imagine a life beyond the limitations of your wounds. Allow this light to play freely through your mind, opening doors you didn't know existed.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Finally, feel all seven lights blending together in your heart center into a luminous, warm, white-gold light. This is Connectedness — the felt sense that all parts of you belong, that no part is excluded or forgotten. This light radiates outward, whispering: "We are all one family. Every part has a place. Every voice matters."

[Pause 20 seconds]

Rest now in this integrated Self energy. All eight qualities alive and present. This is who you truly are beneath the protective layers. Take a moment to simply be in this fullness.

[Pause 30 seconds]

Send a wave of appreciation to all of your parts — the protectors, the managers, the firefighters, and especially your wounded inner child parts. Let each part know: "I see you. I appreciate you. I am here now."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Create an anchor for this Self energy — a gesture you can use to access it quickly. Perhaps pressing thumb and forefinger together, or placing your hand on your heart. Practice this three times: make the gesture, feel the energy, release.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Take three grounding breaths. Feel your body. Wiggle your fingers and toes. When you're ready, gently open your eyes. This Self energy is always within you — a lantern that can never be extinguished.

A3. MEETING YOUR INNER CHILD
Filename: meeting-inner-child.mp3
Duration: 18 minutes
Type: Guided Meditation (Beginner)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Create a safe space in your mind — a place where only goodness can enter. Perhaps it's a sunlit garden, a cozy room, or a quiet beach. Take a moment to fill in the details. What colors do you see? What does the air feel like? What sounds are present?

[Pause 15 seconds]

When your safe space feels vivid and real, gently set an intention to meet your inner child. You don't need to force anything — simply hold the intention with warmth and openness.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Notice if there's a shift in the atmosphere. Perhaps the light changes, or you feel a flutter in your chest. Trust whatever you notice.

Your inner child may appear from a path, or simply materialize nearby. They may come readily or hang back cautiously. Either response is completely natural.

[Pause 15 seconds]

As your inner child becomes visible, take a moment to really see them. Notice how old they are. What are they wearing? What expression do they carry? Notice their posture. Whatever you see, meet it with gentle, open curiosity.

From your heart, send them warmth: "I see you. You are welcome exactly as you are."

[Pause 20 seconds]

Move toward your inner child gently — only as close as they seem comfortable with. If they're wary, sit down at a respectful distance. If they seem open, kneel so you're at their eye level.

Look into their eyes with love and compassion. Speak softly: "Hello, little one. I'm so glad to see you. I know things have been hard for you. I'm here now."

[Pause 20 seconds]

Ask gently: "What do you need right now? What would help you feel safe?" Then simply listen. The answer might come as words, images, feelings, or a deep knowing.

Perhaps they need a hug. Perhaps they need to hear: "It wasn't your fault" or "You are lovable exactly as you are." Perhaps they simply need your quiet, steady presence.

Offer whatever arises naturally. Trust your Self to know exactly what is needed.

[Pause 30 seconds]

If your inner child allows you to be close, hold them gently. Feel their small body against yours. You might rock them gently, or stroke their hair, or simply hold them. Speak the words they've been longing to hear: "I love you. You are safe now. You don't have to carry this alone anymore."

[Pause 20 seconds]

Before we close, give your inner child a gift — a symbol of your love. Perhaps a small glowing light, a special stone, a soft toy, or a tiny star for their pocket. Tell them: "Whenever you hold this, remember I love you and I am always with you."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Say goodbye for today — not a permanent goodbye, but a promise of return: "I will come back. I will visit you here. You matter to me more than you know."

Give them a final embrace or wave. Turn and walk slowly back along the path, feeling the golden thread of connection between you that can never be broken.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Gently return your awareness to the room. Feel the surface beneath you. Take a grounding breath. When you're ready, open your eyes.

What does your inner child need to hear from you? Take a moment to listen with your heart. What words were never spoken? What comfort was never given?

[Pause 15 seconds]

Perhaps they need to hear: "You are enough, exactly as you are." Speak these words now, silently or aloud, and feel the truth of them resonating through your body.

[Pause 10 seconds]

[Pause 10 seconds]

Perhaps they need to hear: "I will never abandon you. I am the adult you grew up to become, and I am strong enough to take care of you now."

[Pause 10 seconds]

[Pause 20 seconds]

See yourself as the parent your inner child always needed — patient, warm, consistent, and endlessly loving. You don't have to be perfect. You just have to be present.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Make a commitment to your inner child: "Every day, I will check in with you. I will listen when you try to get my attention through feelings or body sensations. I will respond with kindness rather than impatience. I will learn to give you what you need."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Hold your inner child close one more time. Feel the warmth between you. This is the most important relationship of your life — the one between you and your own precious child within.

[Pause 20 seconds]

Gently begin to return to the present. Carry this loving connection with you as you open your eyes and move through your day.

A6. UNBLENDING PRACTICE
Filename: unblending.mp3
Duration: 15 minutes
Type: Technique (Intermediate)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

In IFS, "blending" happens when a part's feelings, thoughts, or impulses take over and you lose your sense of Self. You become the anger, the fear, or the shame — rather than noticing that a part of you is feeling angry, afraid, or ashamed.

Unblending is the gentle process of creating separation between you and the part, so you can relate to it from Self rather than being consumed by it.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Begin by noticing which part is present right now. You don't need to go looking — simply notice what's already here. Perhaps there's anxiety, sadness, frustration, self-criticism, or restlessness. Whatever it is, acknowledge it: "I notice that a part of me is feeling..."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now, here's the key question: ask this part if it would be willing to give you just a little bit of space. Not to go away — that would feel threatening to the part. Simply to step back slightly, like moving from being nose-to-nose to sitting across a room.

You might say: "I see you, and I want to understand you better. Could you give me just a little room so I can be with you more clearly?"

[Pause 15 seconds]

Notice what happens. Feel the difference between being the emotion and being WITH the emotion. Even a small amount of separation is significant. You might notice that the feeling is still there, but you now have a bit of breathing room around it.

[Pause 15 seconds]

From this place of slight separation, you can now relate to the part with curiosity: "What are you trying to protect me from? What do you need me to know?"

[Pause 15 seconds]

If you find yourself getting pulled back in — blending again — that's completely normal. Simply notice it, take a breath, and gently ask the part to step back once more. Unblending is a practice, not a perfect performance.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take three grounding breaths. Feel your body. Notice the increased clarity that comes with even small amounts of unblending. This is Self energy — and it's always available to you.

A7. PARTS COUNCIL MEDITATION
Filename: parts-council.mp3
Duration: 30 minutes
Type: Guided Meditation (Advanced)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Find a comfortable position and allow your eyes to close. Take several deep breaths to settle into a state of calm, clear Self energy.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Imagine a beautiful, peaceful gathering space within you. Perhaps it's a round table in a warm room, a circle of comfortable chairs in a garden, or a campfire under the stars. This is where your parts can come together safely.

[Pause 10 seconds]

From Self, you are the host of this gathering. You sit in the center, holding space with curiosity, compassion, and calm. You are not here to judge, fix, or silence anyone. You are here to listen.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Gently invite your parts to join the council. They may come one at a time or several at once. Perhaps your inner critic appears first — taking a seat with arms crossed. Perhaps a worried part slips in quietly. Perhaps an angry part stands at the edge, not yet sure if it's safe to sit down.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Welcome each part as it arrives: "Thank you for being here. Your presence matters. You are welcome exactly as you are." Notice who comes. Notice who hangs back.

[Pause 20 seconds]

When the gathering feels settled, begin by inviting one part to speak. Ask: "Who would like to share first? What would you like the rest of us to know?"

[Pause 15 seconds]

Listen deeply as this part shares. You might hear complaints, fears, frustrations, or requests. Don't interrupt. Don't argue. Simply listen with your whole being and reflect back what you hear: "I hear you saying that you feel... and you need..."

[Pause 20 seconds]

After this part has spoken, ask if any other part has a response. Perhaps another part disagrees, or feels triggered, or wants to add something. Let the dialogue unfold naturally, with you as the compassionate moderator ensuring every voice is heard.

[Pause 20 seconds]

Continue inviting parts to speak. Some may surprise you. The anxious part might reveal deep wisdom. The angry part might be protecting something tender. The controlling part might be exhausted and ready to share the burden.

[Pause 30 seconds]

As the council unfolds, look for moments of connection between parts. Perhaps two parts who have been in conflict realize they share the same goal — protecting you from pain. Perhaps a protector softens when it sees the vulnerability of the inner child it guards.

[Pause 20 seconds]

Before closing the council, ask: "Is there anything anyone still needs to say? Any part who hasn't been heard?" Give space for any final words.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Thank each part for their courage and honesty. Let them know: "This council is always available. We can meet again whenever anyone needs to be heard. You are all part of this family, and every voice matters."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Watch as the parts begin to settle, some staying in the gathering space, others returning to their usual places within you. Notice how you feel — perhaps more integrated, more aware of your inner world, more connected to the complexity and beauty of your internal family.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take three grounding breaths. Feel your body in the room. When you're ready, gently open your eyes.

A8. WORKING WITH FIREFIGHTERS
Filename: firefighter-work.mp3
Duration: 20 minutes
Type: Technique (Intermediate)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

In IFS, Firefighter parts are the ones who spring into action when your inner child's pain threatens to break through. They use impulsive strategies — binge eating, drinking, scrolling, rage, dissociation, self-harm — not because they're bad, but because they're desperately trying to put out a fire of unbearable emotion.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Begin by acknowledging your firefighter parts. Turn inward and notice: are there any parts of you that act first and think later? Parts that take over when emotions get too intense?

[Pause 15 seconds]

When you identify one, approach it with gratitude rather than shame: "Thank you for protecting me. I know you step in when things feel unbearable. Your job is incredibly hard, and I appreciate you."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Your firefighter part may be surprised by this response. It's probably used to being criticized, shamed, or forced into hiding. Stay with it. Let it feel your genuine appreciation.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now ask gently: "What fire are you trying to put out? What emotion or memory would overwhelm me if you didn't step in?"

[Pause 20 seconds]

Listen to the answer. Beneath every firefighter's behavior is a vulnerable inner child part carrying pain that feels unsurvivable. The firefighter isn't the problem — it's a symptom of pain that hasn't been witnessed yet.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Ask your firefighter: "What do you need from me so that you don't have to work so hard? What would help you trust that I can handle the emotions you're protecting me from?"

[Pause 20 seconds]

Most firefighters need to see that your Self is strong enough to be with the inner child's pain without falling apart. They need evidence that there's a competent, compassionate leader in charge. You can start building this trust right now, in this moment, simply by staying present and curious.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Let your firefighter know: "I'm learning to be with difficult feelings. I'm building my capacity. You don't have to retire — but maybe we can work as a team. You can let me try first, and you can step in only if I really need you."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take three deep breaths. Thank your firefighter one more time. Open your eyes when you're ready.

A9. BOX BREATHING
Filename: box-breathing.mp3
Duration: 10 minutes
Type: Breathing Exercise (Beginner)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Box breathing is a simple, powerful technique to calm your nervous system. We'll breathe in a square pattern: inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4.

Find a comfortable position. Let your eyes close. Take a natural breath to begin.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Inhale slowly through your nose... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

Exhale slowly through your mouth... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

[Continue this pattern]

Inhale... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

Exhale... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four.

[Continue for 8-10 full cycles with calm, steady pacing]

Notice how your body feels. Perhaps your heartbeat has slowed. Perhaps your muscles have softened. Perhaps your mind feels clearer.

[Pause 10 seconds]

You can use box breathing anytime you feel stressed, anxious, or overwhelmed. Even two or three cycles can shift your nervous system from fight-or-flight into calm and clarity.

When you're ready, let your breath return to its natural rhythm. Open your eyes.

A10. 4-7-8 BREATHING
Filename: 4-7-8-breathing.mp3
Duration: 12 minutes
Type: Breathing Exercise (Intermediate)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The 4-7-8 breathing technique is a powerful method for deep relaxation. We'll inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, and exhale for 8. The extended exhale activates your body's relaxation response.

Find a comfortable position. Let your eyes close.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Place the tip of your tongue gently against the roof of your mouth, just behind your front teeth. It will stay here throughout the practice.

Exhale completely through your mouth, making a gentle whoosh sound.

Now:

Inhale quietly through your nose... two... three... four.

Hold your breath... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

Exhale completely through your mouth with a whoosh... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[Pause 3 seconds]

Again:

Inhale... two... three... four.

Hold... two... three... four... five... six... seven.

Exhale slowly... two... three... four... five... six... seven... eight.

[Continue for 6-8 full cycles with calm pacing]

Feel the deep relaxation spreading through your body with each cycle. Your muscles softening. Your mind quieting. Your whole system settling into peace.

[Pause 15 seconds]

This technique is especially powerful before sleep or during moments of acute stress. Even four cycles can produce a noticeable shift in your body and mind.

Let your breath return to its natural rhythm. Feel your body. When you're ready, gently open your eyes.

B1. CULTIVATING SELF ENERGY PRACTICE
From: Module 1 — IFS Foundations
Duration: ~20 minutes
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Welcome to this Cultivating Self Energy practice. Begin by finding a comfortable position — you may sit upright in a supportive chair with your feet flat on the floor, or lie down on your back with your arms resting gently at your sides. Allow your body to settle into whatever surface supports you. Feel the weight of your body being held. There is nothing you need to do right now, nowhere you need to be. Take a moment to honor yourself for choosing this time for your inner healing. If at any point during this meditation anything feels too intense, know that you can open your eyes, feel your feet on the ground, and return fully to the room. You are always in control.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Let us begin with your breath — the bridge between your outer world and your inner landscape. Take a slow, deep breath in through your nose, filling your belly first, then your ribs, then your chest... hold gently at the top for a count of three... and now release fully through your mouth with an audible sigh, letting go of anything you no longer need. Again — breathe in deeply, feeling your body expand with fresh, nourishing air... hold... and release, feeling your shoulders drop, your jaw soften, your hands uncurl. One more time — a deep, slow inhale, drawing in peace and presence... hold... and a long, slow exhale, releasing tension, worry, and the busyness of the day.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now allow your breath to return to its natural rhythm. Begin to bring your awareness inward, like dimming the lights in a room so you can see the stars outside. Notice the surface beneath you — the texture, the temperature, the gentle pressure against your body. Feel your feet, your legs, your hips, your spine, your shoulders, your head — all being held and supported. Scan slowly through your body from the crown of your head down to the tips of your toes. Simply notice what is present without trying to change anything. Perhaps there is tension in your shoulders, a tightness in your chest, a heaviness in your belly. Whatever you find, greet it with a gentle nod of acknowledgment. These sensations are messages from your parts, and you are here to listen.

[Pause 15 seconds]

As you settle deeper into this quiet inner space, I invite you to notice your internal landscape. Imagine that within you there is a vast, open space — like a sunlit meadow, a quiet temple, or a calm ocean. This is the space where your Self naturally resides. Your Self is not something you need to create or earn — it is your essential nature, the wise and loving core that has always been within you. As your parts begin to notice your presence here, some may feel curious, some may feel cautious, and some may feel relieved. Simply let them know that you are here, that you are settling in, and that everyone is welcome.

[Pause 10 seconds]

We will now begin to access each of the eight qualities of Self energy, one at a time. Imagine each quality as a luminous light that already lives within you, waiting to be noticed and awakened. As we move through each one, allow yourself to feel it in your body, see it in your mind's eye, and let it gently fill your entire being. There is no right or wrong way to experience these qualities — simply remain open and curious about whatever arises.

[Pause 5 seconds]

[Pause 20 seconds]

[Pause 20 seconds]

Take several deep breaths. Thank all parts who participated in this sacred ceremony. When you are ready, gently open your eyes.

Did you learn to amplify your distress — crying louder, clinging tighter, escalating your emotions — because that was the only way to get a response? This is the anxious pattern, and the parts that carry it are trying desperately to ensure that connection is never lost.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Or did you learn to suppress your needs — to stop crying, to handle things on your own, to act as though you did not need anyone — because expressing vulnerability led to rejection or dismissal? This is the avoidant pattern, and the parts that carry it are protecting you from the pain of reaching out and being met with emptiness.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Or did you experience a confusing mix — sometimes your caregiver was warm and present, sometimes they were frightening or unpredictable? Did you learn to approach and withdraw simultaneously, never quite knowing whether closeness was safe or dangerous? This is the disorganized pattern, and the parts that carry it are caught between two competing survival drives: the need for connection and the need for self-protection.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Or perhaps you were fortunate enough to have at least one caregiver who was consistently warm, responsive, and reliable. If so, you likely developed a secure base from which to explore the world. Even if your attachment is mostly secure, you may still have parts that carry relational wounds from specific experiences.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Whatever pattern you identified, I want you to know: this strategy kept you alive. It helped you navigate an environment that demanded adaptation. Turn toward the parts that carry this pattern and say: "Thank you. You figured out how to survive. That took intelligence, courage, and resilience. I am grateful for you."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Now, bring your awareness to the present. How does this early pattern show up in your adult relationships? Take a moment to reflect honestly. Do you find yourself anxiously seeking reassurance from partners or friends, needing constant confirmation that the relationship is secure? Do you pull away when people get close, finding reasons to create distance or convincing yourself you do not really need anyone? Do you oscillate between desperate closeness and sudden withdrawal, confusing both yourself and others? Notice without judgment. Simply observe.

[Pause 20 seconds]

As you observe these patterns, you may notice specific parts activating. Perhaps an anxious part tightens in your chest. Perhaps an avoidant part creates a cool distance in your mind. Perhaps a young, confused part feels a swirl of contradictory impulses. Whatever arises, stay in Self. Say: "I see you. I understand why you do what you do. And I am here now — a secure base within myself that you can rely on."

[Pause 15 seconds]

This is the profound insight of attachment work combined with IFS: you can become your own secure base. Your Self — calm, compassionate, consistent, and always present — can offer your attachment-wounded parts exactly what they needed from an external caregiver. You do not have to wait for the perfect partner, the perfect friend, or the perfect therapist to feel securely attached. You can begin right now, from within.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take a moment to offer your attachment-wounded parts the experience of secure connection. Imagine your Self as a warm, steady presence — like a lighthouse on a rocky shore. Your parts are boats on the water, sometimes tossed by storms, sometimes drifting in calm seas. But the lighthouse is always there, always shining, always guiding them home. Say to your parts: "I am here. I am not going anywhere. You can venture out and explore, and when you need to return, I will be right here, shining for you."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Before we close, set one small intention for your relationships this week. Perhaps it is to notice when an attachment pattern activates and to pause before reacting. Perhaps it is to practice reaching out for connection when your avoidant parts want to withdraw. Perhaps it is to reassure your anxious parts from Self rather than seeking reassurance externally. Choose something specific, achievable, and kind.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take three slow, grounding breaths. Feel your body in the present moment. Wiggle your fingers and toes. Notice the sounds around you. When you are ready, gently open your eyes. You carry within you the capacity for secure attachment — not because your early experiences were perfect, but because your Self is wise enough, loving enough, and steady enough to offer what was missing. Your attachment story is not finished. The next chapter is being written by you, from Self, right now.

[Pause 10 seconds]


------------------------------------------------------------------------
B11. BEFRIENDING YOUR INNER CRITIC
From: Module 10 — Maintaining Your Healing & Moving Forward
Duration: ~20 minutes
Type: Guided Dialogue with the Critic Part
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Welcome to this Befriending Your Inner Critic practice. Of all the parts in your internal family, the Inner Critic is often the most misunderstood. It is the voice that tells you that you are not good enough, that you should have done better, that other people are judging you, that you will inevitably fail. Most people spend their lives trying to silence, override, or defeat this voice. But in IFS, we take a radically different approach. We turn toward the Critic with curiosity and compassion, recognizing that beneath its harsh words is a part that is working desperately to protect you. Today, we will befriend this part — not to empower its criticism, but to understand its true intention so it can find a new, less painful role in your system. Find a comfortable position. Allow your eyes to close. Take three centering breaths.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Settle into your body and connect with your Self energy. Feel the calm, clear, compassionate core within you. You will need these qualities today, because the Inner Critic can be a powerful part, and approaching it requires steady Self leadership. Remind yourself: you are not here to fight the Critic or to submit to it. You are here to understand it. From Self, you have the wisdom and the love to be with whatever this part reveals.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now, gently turn your attention inward and invite the Inner Critic to make itself known. You might notice it as a voice in your head — perhaps sharp and commanding, perhaps quiet and insidious, perhaps sounding like a specific person from your past. You might notice it as a feeling — a tightening in your stomach, a heaviness in your chest, a tension in your jaw. You might notice it as an image — perhaps a stern figure, a pointing finger, a looming shadow. However it appears, acknowledge its presence: "I know you are here. I would like to talk with you today."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Before you begin the dialogue, do a Self-energy check. Ask yourself: "How do I feel toward this Critic part right now?" If you notice anger, frustration, fear, or the urge to get rid of it — those are other parts reacting to the Critic. That is completely natural. Gently ask those reactive parts to step back slightly so you can approach the Critic from Self. Say to them: "I understand your feelings about the Critic. I need a little space so I can talk with it directly. I promise I will not let it take over." Wait until you can feel genuine curiosity about the Critic before proceeding.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Now, from Self, turn toward the Critic and begin the conversation. Start with appreciation — this will likely surprise the Critic, which is accustomed to being fought or feared. Say: "I know you have been working very hard. I can see how much energy you put into trying to keep me in line, trying to make me better, trying to prevent me from being hurt. That is an exhausting job, and I want you to know that I see your effort."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Notice how the Critic responds. It may be suspicious — "What are you up to?" It may be relieved — "Finally, someone notices." It may intensify — launching into a list of your faults as if to prove why its job is necessary. Whatever happens, stay in Self. Stay curious. Stay compassionate.

[Pause 10 seconds]

Now ask the most important question in all of IFS work: "What are you trying to protect me from? What would happen if you stopped criticizing me?" This question reaches past the Critic's behavior to its core intention. Listen carefully to what it reveals.

[Pause 20 seconds]

Most Inner Critics are trying to protect you from one or more of these outcomes: rejection — "If I make you perfect, no one will leave you." Failure — "If I push you harder, you will succeed and be safe." Shame — "If I criticize you first, it will hurt less when others do." Vulnerability — "If I keep you on guard, no one can catch you off guard." Notice which of these resonates most strongly with your Critic. Behind every critical voice is a frightened child part that the Critic is desperately trying to shield from pain.

[Pause 15 seconds]

Ask your Critic: "Who are you protecting? Can you show me the younger part that would be hurt if you stopped your job?" This is a pivotal moment. The Critic may point you toward an exile — a young, wounded part carrying shame, fear of abandonment, or the belief that they are fundamentally flawed. You do not need to work with the exile right now. Simply acknowledge it: "I see the little one you are protecting. I understand now why you work so hard."

[Pause 15 seconds]

Turn back to the Critic and say: "I want to make you an offer. You have been doing this job alone for a very long time, and it has cost you. You are exhausted. Your methods cause pain — not because you intend to hurt, but because criticism was the only tool you had. I am here now, and I have other tools. I have compassion, patience, wisdom, and strength. What if we worked as a team? What if you could let me take the lead with the younger parts, and you could have a new role — one that does not require you to be harsh?"

[Pause 15 seconds]

Notice the Critic's response. It may be cautious — it has been in charge for a long time and letting go of control is frightening. It may ask for proof that you can handle the job. It may need to see you successfully care for the vulnerable parts before it trusts you. All of this is natural and healthy. Do not rush the process. Say: "I understand your hesitation. You do not have to change overnight. We can do this gradually. I just want you to know that there is another way, and I am willing to show you."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Before we close, I invite you to offer the Critic a vision of what its new role could look like. Instead of a harsh taskmaster, it could become a wise advisor — still noticing areas for growth, but communicating with encouragement rather than condemnation. Instead of saying "You are so stupid," it could say "That was a mistake, and you can learn from it." Instead of saying "No one will ever love you," it could say "You are worthy of love, and here is how we can open to it." Ask the Critic: "How does that sound? Would you be willing to try?"

[Pause 15 seconds]

Whatever the Critic's answer — whether enthusiastic, cautious, or skeptical — honor it. This is just the beginning of a new relationship. Thank the Critic for showing up today, for sharing its fears and intentions, for being willing to engage in dialogue rather than simply broadcasting from behind a wall. Say: "Thank you for talking with me today. I see you differently now — not as an enemy, but as a protector who has been doing an impossible job with limited tools. I will come back. We will keep talking. And together, we will find a way that does not require either of us to suffer."

[Pause 10 seconds]

Take three slow, deep breaths. Feel your body in the room. Notice the shift in your relationship with your Inner Critic — perhaps subtle, perhaps profound. Wiggle your fingers and toes. When you are ready, gently open your eyes.

[Pause 5 seconds]

As you move through your day, notice when the Critic's voice arises. Instead of fighting it or obeying it, try a third option: pause, take a breath, and say internally: "I hear you. I know you are trying to help. Let me take this one." Over time, this simple practice transforms the Critic from a tyrant into an ally — and that transformation changes everything.

[Pause 10 seconds]

D1. ATTACHMENT STYLE ASSESSMENT INTRODUCTION
Filename: attachment-assessment-intro.mp3
Duration: 3 minutes
Type: Guided Introduction (Beginner)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Welcome. Before you begin this assessment, I'd like to take a few moments to help you settle in and understand what we'll be exploring together.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Take a slow, deep breath in through your nose... and release it gently through your mouth. Allow your body to soften. There are no right or wrong answers here — only your honest experience.

[Pause 5 seconds]

This assessment explores your attachment style — the way you tend to connect with the people closest to you. Attachment styles are patterns that form early in life, shaped by how your caregivers responded to your needs as a young child. Were your needs met consistently? Were they sometimes ignored or met unpredictably? Were you encouraged to be close, or did closeness feel unsafe?

[Pause 5 seconds]

These early experiences created a kind of blueprint — an internal map your nervous system still follows today. It influences how you seek closeness, how you handle conflict, how comfortable you feel depending on others, and how you respond when you fear someone might leave.

[Pause 5 seconds]

There are four main attachment styles. Secure attachment, where closeness feels natural and safe. Anxious attachment, where you crave connection but worry it might disappear. Avoidant attachment, where independence feels safer than vulnerability. And disorganized attachment, where you long for closeness but also feel afraid of it.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Most people carry a blend of these patterns, and none of them are permanent. Understanding your style is not a label — it is a lantern. It illuminates why certain relationships feel easy and others feel painful. It helps you see which protective parts activate when intimacy gets close, and which wounded parts are driving the need.

[Pause 5 seconds]

As you move through the questions, try to answer from your gut feeling rather than what you think the "right" answer should be. Think about your patterns in close relationships — romantic partners, close friends, family. Notice what comes up without judging it.

[Pause 5 seconds]

If a question stirs something uncomfortable, that's okay. Place a hand on your heart and remind yourself: "This is just information. It helps me understand myself better. I am safe right now."

[Pause 5 seconds]

Remember — your attachment patterns developed for good reasons. They were your young self's best strategy for staying connected and staying safe. By understanding them now, you open the door to earning a more secure attachment from within, using the Self energy and inner child work you are already learning.

[Pause 5 seconds]

Take one more deep breath. When you feel ready, begin the assessment with openness and self-compassion. There is no grade, no judgment — only insight.

You are ready. Let's begin.
